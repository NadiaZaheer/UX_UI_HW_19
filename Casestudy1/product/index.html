My Journey Towards the Redesign of
The US Department of Education Website


This case study showcases the redesign of the US Department of Education website, with a focus on improving user experience and addressing navigation and information hierarchy issues.
The project also involved redesigning the homepage to reduce clutter and enhance visual appeal, resulting in a more intuitive and user-friendly website.

Project Overview:

The US Department of Education serves as a vital resource hub for educators, students, parents, and policymakers.It provides information, resources, and facilitates communication within the education community. However, the existing website faced usability challenges, particularly with navigation and finding information. The goal of my redesign project was to create a more user-friendly and intuitive website. By streamlining the navigation system, reducing clutter, and improving the visual appeal, the objective was to enhance the overall user experience. I employed a user-driven design approach, incorporating insights from research and testing to align the website with users' needs and expectations.

What was the Problem ?

Users were experiencing difficulty in finding the required information on the US Department of Education website due to its confusing navigation system and complex information hierarchy. The website's complexity and cluttered homepage further exacerbated the problem, making it challenging for users to navigate and access relevant information effectively.

The Solution:

Redesigning the website, focusing on improving the information architecture, navigation system, and homepage. By simplifying the navigation and optimizing the homepage's layout and visual appeal,creating a more user-friendly experience that facilitates easy access to information.


So the challenges were...

  • Complex navigation system leading to difficulty in finding information.
  • Data-heavy content overwhelming users.
  • Users struggling with the website's usability and overall navigation.

The Design Process


Understanding Our Users...

I started by conducting user research to understand pain points and challenges faced by the users. Gathering insights through interviews & usability testing of the current website helped me to identify key user needs and expectations.Here are some findings...



Analyzing the Current Website...

Then I moved towards the analysis of the existing information architecture and navigation system of the website,annoted heuristic/usability issues,and identified areas for improvement based on user feedback and usability testing.

Brainstorming & Ideation

After gathering valuable insights from user research and conducting a thorough analysis, I transitioned into the ideation phase. To kickstart the ideation process, I created Proto Personas, which represented the key user archetypes based on the research findings.

Wireframing & Prototyping

  • Building upon the ideation phase, I moved forward with translating the design concepts into wireframes and prototypes.
  • To ensure a well-structured and intuitive user experience, I initially focused on the navigation system design.
  • Through conducting card sorting exercises, I refined the site map and navigation, organizing the content to align with user expectations and enhance information accessibility.
  • Using the wireframes, I developed lo-fi clickable prototypes.

Interactive Components:

  • These interactive prototypes simulated user interactions, enabling me to test the usability of the design solutions.
  • Incorporating user feedback, I iterated on the prototypes to refine the user experience further.
  • After testing the lo-fi prototype, I designed interactive navigation components to improve usability.
  • I refined the navigation structure and implemented interactive features like dropdown menus and hover effects.

Design Exploration: Color Palette and RWD Homepage Redesign

  • I proceeded to redesigning the homepage to reduce clutter and improve visual appeal.
  • As part of the redesign process, I carefully selected a color palette to enhance the visual appeal and create a cohesive brand identity.
  • I carefully selected a color palette for the project that incorporated shades of blue and aqua, along with complementary white and black.
  • The use of these colors was intentional, as they evoke a sense of calmness, professionalism, and trustworthiness.
  • To ensure readability and contrast, I incorporated yellow text elements that added vibrancy and attention-grabbing accents.

Testing & Iterations

Then I conducted usability testing sessions with target users to evaluate the effectiveness of the redesigned website. Valuable feedback was gathered, leading to iterative design improvements and enhanced user experience.

Final Design





Outcomes and Impact

  • The redesigned website successfully addressed the navigation and information finding challenges.
  • Users reported improved ease in finding the required information, resulting in enhanced user satisfaction.
  • The streamlined navigation system and visually appealing design contributed to a more intuitive and user-friendly experience.

Conclusion:

Through a user-centered design approach and iterative design process, the US Department of Education website was successfully redesigned to address usability challenges. The project exemplified the significance of understanding user needs and aligning design solutions accordingly to create effective and user-friendly digital experiences. The outcomes demonstrated the value of user research, usability testing, and an iterative design process in improving the overall user experience.
